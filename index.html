sun
The Sun is a massive star at the center of our solar system, 
 made mostly of hydrogen and helium. 
  It generates energy through nuclear fusion, providing heat and 
   light to Earth, which is essential for life.

Mercury
      Mercury is the smallest and closest planet to the Sun in our 
        solar system. It has extreme temperatures, ranging from scorching
         highs during the day to freezing lows at night due to its lack of atmosphere.
          Mercury's surface is rocky and cratered, resembling our Moon.
           It has the shortest orbit, taking just 88 Earth days to circle the Sun.
Venus
Venus, the hottest planet, has a thick atmosphere of carbon dioxide, 
  trapping heat and creating surface temperatures around 475°C (900°F).
  Its surface, hidden beneath clouds of sulfuric acid, is covered in
 volcanoes and plains. Venus has an unusual backward rotation, making its 
  day longer than its year. 
  The extreme conditions make it a challenging target for exploration.
Earth
            Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only known
             world with life. Its diverse landscapes include oceans, forests,
              deserts, and polar ice caps. The atmosphere protects and nurtures
               life, making Earth uniquely habitable in our solar system.
Mars
    Mars, the fourth planet from the Sun, is known for its reddish,
   desert-like landscape. It has a thin atmosphere of mostly carbon 
  dioxide and extreme temperatures. Scientists are exploring Mars
    for signs of ancient water and potential life.
Jupiter
          Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system, with colorful 
            cloud bands and the iconic Great Red Spot storm. Made mostly of hydrogen 
            and helium, it has over 70 moons, including icy Europa. Its strong magnetic 
            field and intense radiation make it a unique gas giant.
Saturn
Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun, famous for its stunning ring system made 
of ice and rock particles. It's a gas giant primarily composed of hydrogen and helium,
with a yellowish hue. Saturn has at least 83 moons, including Titan, which has a thick
     atmosphere and liquid methane lakes.
Uranus
Uranus, the seventh planet from the Sun, spins uniquely on its side.
  Known as an "ice giant," it has a blue-green color due to methane in its
  atmosphere. Uranus also has faint rings and extremely cold temperatures.
Neptune
Neptune is the eighth planet from the Sun, known for its deep blue color
     and powerful winds. It has a faint ring system and 14 moons,
       with Triton being the largest Located about 30 times 
       farther from the Sun than Earth, its classified as an ice giant.
