Sagittarius A*
              The supermassive black hole at the center of the Milky Way.
                       Its about 4.3 million times the mass of our Sun and 
                             located about 26,000 light-years from Earth.
Cygnus X-1
               One of the first black holes ever discovered. 
                It's part of a binary system with a massive companion star,
                 around 6,000 light-years from Earth
V616 Monocerotis (A0620–00)
               This stellar-mass black hole is about 3,300 light-years
                 away and has a mass around 6-12 times that of the Sun.
GRO J1655–40
               Another stellar-mass black hole in a binary system, 
                located about 11,000 light-years away, 
                with a mass of around 5-7 times the Sun’s mass.
